Editor's Corner

Hi, my name is Cale (K4HCK) and I'm the editor of Amateur Radio Weekly.

A big thank you to everyone that has emailed me links over the last few weeks. It makes my job easier, but more importantly it helps surface all the great things happening in Amateur Radio. Keep the suggestions coming!

The most-clicked link from last week's newsletter was the story from RadioWorld about an FM transmission line being snapped and cleared in broad daylight. Pretty wild!

What caught my eye this week was the video from KM4ACK of a MeshCore repeater being installed 200 feet up a tower on the highest point in this area. The repeater has completely changed the MeshCore landscape. While previously I received sporadic localized traffic from my attic-mounted Heltec v3, now I'm hearing both sides of conversations within a 30-50 mile radius and well beyond. In fact, it's been so noisy, I've had to mute MeshCore notifications on my phone. Watching this mode advance so quickly has been exciting and it's all a community effort.
